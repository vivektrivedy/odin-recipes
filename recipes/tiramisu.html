Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is an Italian dessert consisting of layers of sponge cake soaked in coffee and brandy or liqueur with powdered chocolate and mascarpone cheese. The cake is usually topped with cocoa powder. The name "Tiramisu" means "pick me up" or "cheer me up" in Italian, referring to the two caffeine-containing ingredients, coffee and cocoa.

Ingredients

  • mascarpone cheese
  • heavy cream
  • espresso or strong coffee
  • ladyfingers
  • cocoa powder

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until pale and fluffy
  2. Add mascarpone cheese and mix until smooth
  3. In a separate bowl, whip heavy cream until stiff peaks form
  4. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture
  5. Dip ladyfingers in espresso and arrange in a single layer in a 9x13 inch dish
  6. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers
  7. Repeat layering with remaining ladyfingers and mascarpone mixture
  8. Dust with cocoa powder and refrigerate for at least 2 hours before serving
